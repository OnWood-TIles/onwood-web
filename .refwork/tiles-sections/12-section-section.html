How this was done — the build brief

Recommended stack

  • Next.js 15 (App Router) + TypeScript
  • Tailwind CSS for the token system
  • GSAP + ScrollTrigger — scroll assembly & pinning
  • Lenis — buttery smooth-scroll
  • React Three Fiber / drei — 3D tile tilt & room
  • Framer Motion — magnetic buttons, page transitions
  • Canvas / WebGL shader — living wood-grain hero
  • Payload CMS (self-hosted, Postgres) — content + admin in one app
  • NextAuth — admin login, roles & permissions

The "how did they do that" list

  • Animated wood-grain shader behind the hero
  • 96-cell floor grid that lifts under the pointer
  • Live floor visualiser — swap finishes on a room
  • Scroll-reveal + count-up statistics
  • Magnetic CTAs & a live 3-way theme switch
  • Caesarstone vision-board builder (drag-drop moodboard)
  • Specials page with live month-end countdown
  • Tile-curtain page transitions & branded loader

Super Admin & content management

A password-protected back-office at /admin where the OnWood team edits everything on the site — no code, no developer. Role-based: Super Admin (full access incl. users & site settings) and Staff (content only). Draft → preview → publish, with an audit log.

  • Statistics — years, m² laid, suburbs (drives the count-ups)
  • Staff profiles — photo, name, role, bio, order
  • Product catalogue — ranges, specs, price, images, status
  • Specials — offer, % off, was/now, stock, start/end (feeds countdown)
  • Shop details — address, hours, phone, email, map, socials
  • Showroom gallery — upload & reorder photos
  • Testimonials — quote, name, suburb, rating
  • Decor & texture library — upload → auto background-removal → PNG assets
  • Homepage content — hero copy, section headings, theme
